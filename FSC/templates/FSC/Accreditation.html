Key Standards and Expectations

1.6: Scholarship and Academic Support

Each chapter is required to have a designated scholarship/academic chairperson who manages academic initiatives. This individual ensures that members have access to resources supporting their scholastic success. Submit Academic Chairperson Information.

2.5: Chapter Advisor Requirements

Chapters must maintain an active relationship with a primary Chapter Advisor who provides guidance, supports recruitment efforts, and completes biannual reports. Advisors are essential to chapter success and must also attend at least one in-person FSC meeting annually. Submit Advisor Accreditation Information | Submit Advisor ID Credentials.

2.7 & 2.8: Social Media and Website Maintenance

Chapters must maintain an active social media presence to enhance public relations and engagement. Additionally, chapters are encouraged to develop and update a website for improved visibility and communication. Submit Social Media Details.

5.1: Community Service

Chapters must log an average of at least five community service hours per member per semester. This metric helps us understand your chapter's impact on the community. Submit Community Service Records.

Philanthropic Efforts

While philanthropic activities are not required for this year’s accreditation, they remain an important aspect of chapter contributions. Record all charitable efforts to ensure an accurate account of your chapter's impact. Submit Philanthropic Activities.

By submitting these forms, your chapter plays a vital role in tracking the collective achievements of our Greek community, ensuring we meet our shared goals for the year.
